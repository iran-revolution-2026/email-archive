This repository is intended to help members of the Iranian diaspora and other concerned individuals easily contact elected representatives in Europe regarding the situation in Iran.

It provides:
- **Public email addresses** of Members of the European Parliament (MEPs), organized by country.
- **Ready-to-use email templates** that can be adapted and sent directly to representatives.

The goal is to lower the barrier for civic engagement and enable coordinated, lawful, and respectful communication with policymakers.

## Repository Structure

- `MEPs/`  
  Contains text files with email addresses of MEPs, grouped by ISO country codes. See the [Country Reference Table](#country-reference-table) below for mapping.
  - `ALL.txt` contains a combined list of all representatives.
  
- `Templates/`  
  Contains sample email templates that users can copy, customize, and send. Templates are written to be factual, concise, and suitable for correspondence with elected officials.

## How to Use

1. **Find your representatives:** Navigate to the `MEPs/` folder and open the file matching your country code (e.g., `DE.txt` for Germany).
2. **Collect addresses:** Copy the email addresses from the file.
3. **Choose a template:** Open a file in the `Templates/` folder that matches your specific concern.
4. **Customize and Send:** Copy the template into your email client, personalize the text (highly recommended), and send it from your personal account.

## Notes

- **Official Data:** All email addresses included are publicly available official contact details.
- **Personalization:** Templates are suggestions only; users are encouraged to personalize messages to increase impact.
- **Code Note:** `FL.txt` is included for Liechtenstein/Regional outreach.
- **Conduct:** Communications should remain respectful and lawful.
- **Privacy:** This repository does not send emails automatically and does not collect any user data.

## Purpose and Scope

This project exists to support peaceful civic participation by making it easier to reach elected representatives and raise awareness of human-rights concerns related to Iranian protests / revolution 2026.
